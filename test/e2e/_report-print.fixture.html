Aurora-6 image classifier — evaluation

Confusion, regression against the prior model, and class-wise quality on the held-out test set — themed end-to-end from the design tokens.

Executive summary

Aurora-6 reaches 91.4% top-1 accuracy on the held-out set, up from 88.1% in v3.8. The gain is concentrated in the Bird and Vehicle classes; the persistent failure mode is Cat ↔ Dog confusion, which the new model worsens slightly. Calibration improved overall but the model is mildly over-confident above the 0.8 bin.

Top-1 accuracy 91.4% +3.3 pts vs v3.8
Macro F1 0.908 +0.041
Worst-class recall 0.79 Cat, −0.02
Expected calibration error 0.043 −0.011 (better)

Ship with a caveat. The headline accuracy is a clear win, but Cat↔Dog recall regressed. Gate deployment on a targeted evaluation of that pair before promoting v4.2 to production.

Finding: retry amplification

Retry amplification was the second effect under review. The legacy consumers retried failed batches whole, so a single poisoned message re-enqueued its entire batch and the batch size itself grew under pressure, which is the classic shape of a retry storm. The migrated pipeline retries at message granularity with jittered backoff and a poison queue after three attempts. During the forced-failure rehearsal the amplification factor — the ratio of delivered attempts to distinct messages — peaked at one point zero eight against a legacy baseline of two point six under the same fault. The poison queue collected the seeded failures within one scheduling window and nothing else, which is the acceptance criterion the runbook states. Finding: cold-start latency

Cold-start latency closed the verification set. A consumer that restarts must rebuild its assignment state before it accepts work, and in the legacy system that rebuild walked the full partition metadata on every start, which put the median restart at forty seconds and the tail past three minutes during deploys. The migrated scheduler snapshots assignment state to the coordination store every window, so a restarting consumer resumes from the last snapshot and reconciles only the delta. Across forty observed restarts the median came down to six seconds and the worst case to nineteen, both inside the deploy budget. The remaining tail is dominated by container image pulls rather than by state rebuild, which moves the optimisation conversation to the platform layer and out of this report's scope.
